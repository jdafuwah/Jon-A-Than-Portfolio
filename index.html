About Me

I am a Web Developer and Management Information Systems (MIS) Professional with over 3 years of working experience in data management and software implementation across diverse humanitarian interventions, including Malaria, Mental Health, Mpox, Neglected Tropical Diseases (NTD), Non-Communicable Diseases (NCD), HIV & AIDS, Etc.

My passion lies in developing software solutions that address pressing humanitarian challenges in Liberia and across Africa. Through my work with the Health Information Systems Unit at the Ministry of Health, I have gained extensive experience in strengthening digital health systems and supporting evidence-based decision-making.

I hold a Bachelor’s degree in Management Information Systems from Starz University. I am currently seeking a scholarship opportunity at McGill University in Montreal, Canada, to pursue a Master’s in Information Studies with a focus on International Development. This academic pursuit will further enhance my ability to contribute meaningfully to Liberia’s digital transformation and national development efforts.

Born and raised in Liberia, I have spent over three years serving as a Data Officer with the Ministry of Health, where I developed a deep appreciation for the role of technology in improving public health outcomes.
My recent journey into web development — working with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript — stems from a passion to help national governments and both international and local NGOs harness digital tools for real-time data management, performance monitoring, and stronger health systems across Liberia and beyond.

So far I've worked with 6+ International NGOs and the Ministry of Health in Liberia to improve health systems and real-time data management.
